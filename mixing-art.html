Art Experiments

Mixing LoRAs's to Interpolate Art Styles Across History

Exploring the intersection of AI and art through historical epoch interpolation

Problem

Modern generative diffusion models are highly flexible, but fine-tuning full models for specific artistic styles is computationally expensive and inefficient. At the same time, combining artistic styles across different historical periods remains an open challenge: How can we merge learned visual styles in a controlled and interpretable way?

Approach

We use Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) applied to the Stable Diffusion v1.4 model. Three LoRAs are trained on WikiArt datasets representing:

  • Early Renaissance
  • Expressionism
  • Pop Art

Each LoRA learns a distinct artistic distribution while keeping the base model frozen. We then perform linear interpolation between LoRA weights to generate mixed-style models.

System Design

  • 1. LoRA Training: Each art era is encoded as a separate LoRA trained on curated WikiArt subsets
    2. Style Merging: We create two interpolation setups:
    V1: Pairwise blending of two art eras
    V2: Three-way blending across all eras
    3. Image Generation: The merged LoRA models are used to generate novel images that combine stylistic features across epochs.

Classification Analysis

  • To evaluate separability of styles, we fine-tuned a ResNet50 classifier to categorize images into the three art eras. The classifier correctly identifies images generated by individual LoRAs. For two-way interpolations, classification remains partially stable. For three-way merges, classification becomes less distinct but still shows structure. This suggests that learned style boundaries persist even under interpolation.

Results

  • The experiments demonstrate that: LoRA enables efficient and modular style learning. Further, linear interpolation produces meaningful hybrid artistic outputs. Art style representations remain partially separable in latent space. Even blended models preserve identifiable stylistic traces

Key Insights

  • Artistic styles are not strictly isolated in diffusion latent space.
  • LoRA weights can be meaningfully interpolated for style blending.
  • Hybrid generations retain recognizable historical features.
  • Classification performance suggests structured but overlapping style representations.
